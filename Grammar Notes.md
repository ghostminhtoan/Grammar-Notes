## A.1 COLLOQUIAL ENGLISH

<a id="unit-1"></a>
### Unit 1

1.  Good morning/afternoon, class.  
    Good morning/afternoon, Mr. Han.
2.  Goodbye, class.  
    Goodbye, Sir/Madam. See you again! So long!
3.  Who's there?  
    It's me. How are you? Fine, thanks. (And you?)
4.  Stand up/Sit down, please.  
    Yes, Sir/Madam.
5.  Who is Nam?  
    Here, I am. / He's absent.
6.  May I go out, Miss Lan?  
    What for? For the toilet/some drink. All right.
7.  Whose is this?  
    It's mine. Here you are. Thank you.
8.  Sing the song: The Alphabet.  
    With great pleasure.
9.  What's your name? / Who are you?  
    My name is Nam. / I'm Nam. How do you spell it/your name/that word? N.A.M.
10. May I ask you a question?  
    Go ahead. Who else can answer this question? Me/I can.

<a id="unit-2"></a>
### Unit 2

1.  Ouch! Your hand/arm/leg/shoe hurts me.
2.  Excuse me! Move aside/Gangway, please.
3.  Don't be distracted/absent-minded. Pay attention to your lesson.
4.  It's not my fault.
5.  What are you doing? / What do you do?
6.  What language(s) do/can you speak?
7.  What do you mean?
8.  Let me join you.
9.  Let me alone! Don't disturb me.
10. Thank you very much. It's very kind of you. You are so kind.
11. I'm much obliged to you!  
    Don't mention it. / You are welcome.
12. How many languages can you speak?

## A.2 COLLOQUIAL ENGLISH

<a id="unit-1-introductions-and-opening-conversations"></a>
### Unit 1: Introductions and Opening Conversations

A. Mrs. Smith, I'd like to introduce a friend of mine, Pierre Dubois.  
B. I'm very glad to meet you.  
C. It's a pleasure to meet you.  
B. What's your impression of the United States?  
A. Oh, I'm just going to take it easy.  
C. It's really different from what I expected.  
B. Don't worry. You'll get used to it in no time.  
C. I hope so.

A. Wendy, I'd like you to meet my brother, Sam.  
B. Pleased to meet you.  
A. How do you do?  
B. What do you think of Dallas?  
C. Well, I'm still feeling a little homesick and so many things seem strange to me.  
B. It always takes time to get used to a new place.  
C. I hope so.

<a id="unit-2-special-greetings"></a>
### Unit 2: Special Greetings

A. Have a nice weekend!  
B. Thank you. Same to you!  
A. Have you got any special plans?  
B. I've been invited over to a friend's. And you? My roommate is having a party.  
B. Have a good time.  
A. Thank you.

A. Merry Christmas!  
B. Thanks. You too!  
A. Do you have anything special?  
B. Well, my family's away and I can't afford to do much. What about you?  
A. Oh, I haven't decided what I'm going to do yet. I'll probably just stay home.  
B. Take it easy.

<a id="unit-3-saying-goodbye"></a>
### Unit 3: Saying Goodbye

A. I'd like to say goodbye to everyone.  
B. What time are you going?  
A. I'm flying home Sunday afternoon.  
B. Well, goodbye, and have a good trip.  
A. Please don't forget to say goodbye to the rest of the family for me.  
B. No, I won't.

A. I just dropped in to say goodbye.  
B. When do you leave?  
A. I'm catching the 11:00 train.  
B. Take care and give my best to your parents.  
A. Goodbye. Remember to look me up if you're ever in Washington.  
B. Yes, I will.

<a id="unit-4-asking-favors"></a>
### Unit 4: Asking Favors

A. Would you mind if I borrowed your car?  
B. For how long?  
A. Until the end of the week.  
B. Yes, I guess that would be all right.  
A. You are so kind. I'll give it back on time.  
I'm sorry, but it's just not possible. Thank you all the same.

A. Do you think you could lend me some of your records?  
B. It really depends on when.  
A. Until next weekend if that's O.K.  
B. Let me think it over and I'll let you know later.  
A. I don't think you'll refuse it.

<a id="unit-5-apologizing"></a>
### Unit 5: Apologizing

A. I'm afraid I spilt coffee on the table cloth.  
A. I beg your pardon.  
B. Come with me. / Follow me. After you, please.  
A. Make yourself at home. / Don't be shy.  
B. What do you call that in English? Please write that word down.

<a id="unit-6-asking-people-to-repeat"></a>
### Unit 6: Asking People to Repeat

B. Oh, don't worry about it.  
B. I said, "Do you want me to help you?"  
A. I want to apologize. Is there anything I can do?  
A. That's very nice of you. I guess I could use some help.  
B. No, forget about it. It's not important.  
B. Just tell me what you'd like me to do.  
A. I feel terrible, but I've just broken your ashtray.  
A. Excuse me. I didn't hear what you said.  
B. It's nothing to get upset about.  
B. I asked you if you wanted some help.  
A. I don't know what to say. I'd like to replace it.  
A. If you wouldn't mind, I could use a hand.  
B. No, don't be silly. I would not dream of letting you do that.  
B. It's no trouble at all. I'll carry the suitcase for you.  
A. It's so kind of you.

<a id="unit-7-giving-compliments"></a>
### Unit 7: Giving Compliments

A. What a beautiful sweater! I like it.  
B. Do you think it looks on me?  
A. Yes, and it goes beautifully with your pants.  
B. You won't believe it, but it was really cheap. I bought it at half price.  
A. You were lucky to find it. I wish I could find one just like it.  
A. That's a very nice jacket. I love that jacket!  
B. Does it really look O.K.?  
A. Yes, and I like the color, too. It matches your hat.  
B. Yes, it fits perfectly. And I got it on sale.  
A. It's incredible. It surely looks expensive.

<a id="unit-8-saying-thank-you"></a>
### Unit 8: Saying Thank You

A. I'd better be going.  
B. But you just got here. Can't you stay a little longer?  
A. I wish I could, but it's already late.  
B. Oh, it's a shame that you have to leave.  
A. Thank you for a wonderful meal.  
B. I'm glad you enjoyed it.  
A. I think it's about time we got going.  
B. Oh, not yet. At least, have one for the road.  
A. No, thanks all the same.  
B. Well, I'm sorry you have to leave so early.  
A. Thank you for a very enjoyable evening.

<a id="unit-9-meeting-old-friends"></a>
### Unit 9: Meeting Old Friends

A. I haven't seen you for ages!  
B. No, I've been in California for the past month.  
A. How nice! Where were you exactly?  
B. I went to visit an uncle of mine in San Francisco. I got back yesterday.  
A. You haven't been around much lately, have you?  
B. No, I've been away on vacation.  
A. Oh, really? Where did you go?  
B. Palm Springs. I've got a cousin there.  
A. May I see your photos taken in California?

<a id="unit-10-complaints"></a>
### Unit 10: Complaints

A. I wish you wouldn't play the TV so loud.  
B. Sorry.  
A. Yes, and while I think of it, please ask when you want to borrow records.  
B. I'm sorry. You're right. I should have asked.  
A. That radio's pretty loud.  
B. I'm sorry. Has it been bothering you?  
A. Yes, and something else. Please don't borrow my clothes without asking.  
B. Sorry. I guess I wasn't thinking. I won't do it again.

<a id="unit-11-asking-about-health"></a>
### Unit 11: Asking About Health

A. How's your father been?  
B. He's been out of work for a couple of days.  
A. Really? What's the matter with him?  
B. He has a bad cough.  
A. Well, tell him to take it easy and that I hope he feels better.  
B. Thanks. I'll tell him.

A. I haven't seen Bob lately. How is he?  
B. He's still pretty sick.  
A. I'm sorry to hear that. What's the matter?  
B. We don't know, but he's going to the doctor tomorrow.  
A. Let me know if there's anything I can do.  
B. I'll tell him. Thank for asking about him.

<a id="unit-12-taking-a-taxi"></a>
### Unit 12: Taking a Taxi

A. Kennedy Airport, please. I have to be there by 7:00.  
B. I can't promise anything, but I'll do my best.  
B. That'll be $7.65, please.  
A. Thank you very much. Here $10. Give me 2 dollars back, please.

A. Grand Central Station, please. I want to try to catch the 6:00 train.  
B. I think you'll make it if we don't get stuck in a traffic jam... This is it. That's $8.15 please.  
A. Thank you. Here's 9 dollars. Keep the change.

<a id="unit-13-at-the-doctor-s-office"></a>
### Unit 13: At the Doctor's Office

A. I have a sore throat and my chest hurts.  
B. How long have you felt like this?  
A. Two or three days now.  
B. I think you've got the flu. There is a lot of it going around.  
A. What do you think I ought to do?  
B. It's nothing serious. Get this prescription filled and go straight to bed.

A. I've got a temperature and my stomach hurts.  
B. How long have you been sick?  
A. It all started the day before yesterday.  
B. You seem to have picked up some kind of infection. Take two days off from work and don't wear yourself out.

<a id="unit-14-at-the-dentist-s-office"></a>
### Unit 14: At the Dentist's Office

A. How long have you felt like this?  
B. It started bothering me yesterday afternoon.  
A. Let me take a look at it.  
B. Can you see anything?  
A. It's a small cavity. It should be easy to fill.  
B. How long have you been in pain?  
A. It started to hurt when I was eating dinner.  
B. Open your mouth as wide as you can, please.  
A. It's quite serious. I'm afraid we'll have to pull the decayed tooth.

<a id="unit-15-at-a-drugstore"></a>
### Unit 15: At a Drugstore

A. Could you fill this prescription for me, please?  
B. I'll take care of it right away.  
A. By the way, what do you suggest for insect bites?  
B. Rub this cream on every four hours.

A. I'd like to have this prescription filled.  
B. You can pick it up in about an hour.  
A. Can you give me something for a rash?  
B. Try this lotion. It's supposed to be very good.

<a id="unit-16-at-breakfast"></a>
### Unit 16: At Breakfast

A. You'd like coffee, wouldn't you?  
B. I think I'd rather have tea this morning.

# Part 2: INTERNATIONAL PHONETIC ALPHABET [I.P.A.]

26 English letters are divided into 2 parts:
1.  5 vowels: a, e, i, o, u
2.  21 consonants: b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, q, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, z
    *   A semi-vowel/consonant: w, y

<a id="syllable"></a>
**Syllable**: vowel sound /a/ - 1 diphthong /ai/
<a id="word"></a>

# Part 3: THE NINE PARTS OF SPEECH

The nine kinds of words used for different purposes in a sentence are called parts of speech.

<a id="i-nouns"></a>
## I. NOUNS
A noun is a word used to name something.

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Concrete nouns**: We can see, touch them.
    *   a/ Proper nouns (always capitalized): John, Bingo, Hanoi...
    *   b/ Collective nouns: youth, company, goods, police, government
    *   c/ Common nouns:
        *   Countable nouns: mother, cat, college, city, capital
        *   Uncountable nouns (mass nouns): water, sugar
2.  **Abstract nouns**:
    *   Countable nouns: ideas, opinions
    *   Uncountable nouns (most of the abstract nouns): happiness, sorrow, soul
    *   Action: revenge, theft
    *   Quality: wisdom, beauty
    *   Status: youth, poverty

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions:**
1.  Subject to a finite verb: *My father came home late.*
2.  Object to a finite verb: *The bird built a nest.*
3.  Direct/indirect object: *She gave her cat some milk. / She gave it/some milk to her cat.*
4.  Cognate object to a finite verb: *They will live a happy life.*
5.  Object to a preposition: *We are waiting for our friends.*
6.  Subjective complement: *My friend is a teacher.*
7.  Objective complement: *Her parents named her Trang.*
8.  Apposition to a noun: *James Watt, a scientist invented the steam engine.*
9.  Adjective equivalent, determine a noun: *(My sister's - Her) book is over there.*
10. Adverbial equivalent modifying a finite verb: *He worked (for) two hours.*

<a id="ii-pronouns"></a>
## II. PRONOUNS
A pronoun is a word used to replace a noun or a noun equivalent.

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Personal pronoun**:
    *   subjects: I, you, he, she, it, one, we, you, they
    *   objects: me, you, him, her, it, one, us, you, them
2.  **Self pronouns (Reflexive-Emphasizing)**: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, oneself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves
3.  **Reciprocal pronouns**: each other, one another
4.  **Possessive pronouns**: mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, yours, theirs
5.  **Interrogative pronouns**: who, whom, whose, what, which
6.  **Indefinite pronouns**: all, some, several, many, much, few
7.  **Demonstrative pronouns**: this, that, these, those, one, ones
8.  **Distributive pronouns**: each, either, neither, any, anyone, none
9.  **Relative/Conjunctive pr.**: who, whom, which, whose, of which

<a id="iii-articles"></a>
## III. ARTICLES
There are two articles in Modern English.

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Indefinite article**: It is used with nouns that have an indefinite or general sense.
    *   A /a/ before a consonant: a report, U hu: a union, a university, O iu: a one-eyed man
    *   An /ən/ before a vowel: an apple, an urn, an orange, a silent H: an honorable man
2.  **Definite article**: It is used with nouns that have a definite or particular meaning.
    *   The /ðə/ before a consonant, an aspirated H (the house), O iu, W (the window), Yi (year), U /ju:/ before a vowel (the animal), a silent H (the honesty of a good citizen).
    *   *Hour giờ, honest lương lành, Honour danh dự, heir người thừa kế.*

<a id="iv-adjectives"></a>
## IV. ADJECTIVES

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Proper adjectives**: Vietnamese people -> People from Vietnam
2.  **Descriptive adjectives**: a good man (quality), an old house (state)
3.  **Numeral adjectives**:
    *   one, two, three, four: cardinal
    *   first, second, third, fourth: ordinal
4.  **Relative adjectives**: whatever, whichever (eg: I may take whichever book I like.)
5.  **Interrogative adjectives**: what, which (eg: Which book do you like best?)
6.  **Possessive adjectives**: my, your, his, her, its, one's, our, your, their
7.  **Indefinite adjectives**: many, few, little, some, no, all, enough, several
8.  **Demonstrative adj.**: this, that, these, those, same, such (eg: Why do I ask such a word?)

**Functions:** An adjective is used:
1.  To determine a noun or a pronoun: *This book is cheaper than that one.*
2.  To qualify a noun or a pronoun: *He's good and helpful to everybody.*

<a id="part-4-the-nine-parts-of-speech"></a>
# Part 4: THE NINE PARTS OF SPEECH

<a id="v-verbs"></a>
## V. VERBS

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Finite verbs** [having meaning, subject, tense]: *He goes to school.*
2.  **Non-finite verbs**:
    *   Infinitive verbs: to go (eg: He doesn't want to go to school.)
    *   Bare infinitive verbs: go (eg: Let him go to school.)
    *   Present participle: going (eg: Going to school, he met Ms Lan.)
    *   Past participle: gone (eg: He hasn't gone to school yet.)
    *   Gerund: going (eg: He thinks of going to school.)

<a id="kinds"></a>
**Kinds:**
a. **Special verbs**:
    1.  **Auxiliary verbs**:
        1/ To be + present participle: continuous form
        2/ To be + past participle: passive voice
        3/ To have + past participle: perfect tense
        4/ don't/doesn't/didn't + bare inf. V.1. [negative form]
        5/ Do/Does/Did + (bare inf. V.1.)? [interrogative form]
    2.  **Defective/Modal V.**: can, may, must, ought to, shall, will, could, might, should, would

b. **Ordinary verbs**:
    1.  **Transitive verbs** + object: *My friend bought an encyclopedia.*
    2.  **Intransitive verbs**: *The child sleeps on a hammock.*

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions**: A verb is used to denote:
1.  An action: *The teacher is explaining the lesson now.*
2.  A state: *The 2 world war was terrible.*
3.  A condition: *One should observe the traffic code.*

<a id="vi-adverbs"></a>
## VI. ADVERBS

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
A/ May trạng thái bất quy tắc sau: always, usually, generally, sometimes, now and then, occasionally, at times, seldom, rarely, scarcely, barely, hardly.
B/ Trạng thường trước tính, trạng từ, nhưng khi bổ nghĩa động từ như sau:
- Trạng từ tần suất (always...) thường đứng trước động từ thường, sau động từ "to be".
- Trạng từ chỉ nơi chốn, thời gian xác định: cuối câu.
- Trạng từ chỉ thời gian bất định: trước động từ hoặc sau đặc từ.
- Trạng từ chỉ cách thức / nơi chốn / xác định.

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions**: An adverb is used to modify:
1.  An adjective: *The cost of living in Tokyo is rather/quite/too/extremely/terribly high.*
2.  An adverb: *He did his exercises very quickly/fast/easily.*
3.  A verb: *He explained the lesson slowly and clearly.*
4.  A clause: *Luckily, the bullet has missed him.*

<a id="positions"></a>
**Positions**:
*   Ordinary verbs: *The singer often sang this song very well in that theatre last month.*
*   Verbs of movement: *He rode downtown/there/to school very slowly today.*
*   With frequency words: *The king left here secretly three times on Sunday.*

<a id="vii-prepositions"></a>
## VII. PREPOSITIONS

<a id="classification"></a>
**Classification:**
1.  **Prepositions of time**: before, after, till (until), since, for, during, by, about, at, on, in, from, to (till), upon...
2.  **Prepositions of place**: at, in, on, over, under, above, below, behind, between, beside, beyond, near...
3.  **Prepositions of manner**: with, without, by, by means of, according to, in spite of, as for, but...
4.  **Prepositions of motion**: about, for, from, to, toward(s), out of, through, against, up, down, by, along, past, into, across, over, (round)...
5.  **Prepositions of cause**: for, of, because of/due to, thanks to, by means of, owing to, through...
6.  **Prepositions of purpose**: for, to, in order to, so as to...

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions**: A preposition is a word placed before a noun or a noun equivalent to show the relation between this element to another one in the sentence.
1.  Nouns: *A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush.*
2.  Pronouns: *There is no difference between him and me.*
3.  Verbs [Gerunds]: *He objects to shaking hands with women.* = *He came forward to shake hands (a purpose)* [to look forward to+(V+ing)]
4.  Adverbs: *He has worked hard from then to/till now.*
5.  Phrases: *He was blamed for having been too late.*
6.  Clauses: *Tell him of what you have done.*

<a id="viii-conjunctions"></a>
## VIII. CONJUNCTIONS

<a id="co-ordinating-conjunctions"></a>
**Co-ordinating conjunctions**:
1.  They connect two parts of speech of the equal rank or two phrases to give a simple sentence.
2.  They connect two Finite Verbs to give a compound sentence.

<a id="subordinating-conjunctions"></a>
**Subordinating conjunctions**: They connect two or more Finite Verbs (one main clause and one or more subordinate clauses) to give a complex sentence.

<a id="i-co-ordinating-conjunctions"></a>
**I. Co-ordinating conjunctions**
Classification: Lien từ tập hợp như sau:
1.  **Addition**: and, then, as well as, both...and, not only...but also/as well, furthermore, moreover, besides, no less than, in addition, what is more.
2.  **Contrast**: but, yet, still, however, nevertheless.
3.  **Choice**: or, or else, nor, neither...nor, otherwise.
4.  **Consequence**: for, so, therefore, hence.

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions**: A conjunction is a word used to join one sentence to another sentence or one word to another word of the same function and part of speech.
1.  A simple sentence: *He's not only foolish, but also obstinate.*
2.  A compound sentence: *He was afraid, and he ran away.*

<a id="subordinating-conjunctions"></a>
**Subordinating conjunctions**
Classification: Liên từ phụ thuộc:
*   **So sánh [comparison]**: as...as, not so (as?)...as, er...than, more...than, less...than
*   **Thời gian [time]**: before, until/till, as soon as, no sooner (p.pt.)...than (s.p.), just as, as, when, once, while, after, since, whenever, by the time, every time
*   **Kết quả [result]**: so +(adj./adv.) + that; so many +(pl.C.noun) + that, so much +(Unc.noun) + that; such+(a, an)+(adj) noun + that
*   **Duyên cớ [cause, reason]**: since, as, because, now that: vì...nên
*   **Mục đích [purpose]**: that/so that/in order that +(can, may, could, might): cốt để, lest+(should)=for fear that: để khỏi
*   **Nhượng bộ [concession]**: (al)though, even (though, if), no matter (how, what, when): mặc dù
*   **Điều kiện [condition]**: if=if only, unless=if not, provided (that), so long as, on condition that
*   **Nơi chốn [place]**: where, wherever, whence=where from, as far as
*   **Thể cách [manner]**: (as though, as if, as) +(past subjunctive): dường như
*   **Giới thiệu [introduction]**: that - Xem thử: whether...or not=if - Cái điều mà: what (eg: Tell him + that I wrote him a letter = what I told you. [what=the thing which])

<a id="functions"></a>
**Functions**: They are used to introduce a subordinate clause: noun clause to the finite verb of the main clause or one of nine kinds of adverbial clauses in a complex sentence.
*   A noun clause: *He admitted that he hadn't been at home last night.*
*   An adverbial clause of...: *He fought bravely because he loved his country. [reason]*

<a id="ix-interjections"></a>
## IX. INTERJECTIONS
An interjection is a word added to a sentence to express sudden feeling or a strong emotion. It has no grammatical function in the sentence.
*   *What! You can't do this exercise!*
*   *My friend, alas! is very lazy.*

<a id="part-6-the-comparison-of-adjectives"></a>
# Part 6: THE COMPARISON OF ADJECTIVES

<a id="a-identification-of-short-and-long-adjectives"></a>
## A. IDENTIFICATION OF SHORT AND LONG ADJECTIVES

<a id="i-short-adjectives"></a>
### I. Short adjectives
1.  **Monosyllable adjectives**: long -> longer -> the longest
    *   big -> bigger -> the biggest
    *   *Except*: drunk, frank, glad, scarce, vague.
2.  **Two-syllable adjectives ending in Y, LE, ET, ER, OW**:
    *   gentle -> gentler -> the gentlest
    *   quiet -> quieter -> the quietest
    *   pretty -> prettier -> the prettiest
    *   clever -> cleverer -> the cleverest
    *   narrow -> narrower -> the narrowest
3.  **Two-syllable adjectives having the main stress on the 2nd syllable**:
    *   polite -> politer -> the politest
    *   severe -> severer -> the severest
    *   sublime -> sublimer -> the sublimest
    *   profound -> profounder -> the profoundest
4.  **Some particular adjectives**:
    *   common -> commoner -> the commonest
    *   pleasant -> pleasanter -> the pleasantest
    *   handsome -> handsomer -> the handsomest

<a id="ii-long-adjectives"></a>
### II. Long Adjectives
Adjectives having two or more syllables:
*   patient -> more patient -> the most patient
*   beautiful -> more beautiful -> the most beautiful
*   intelligent -> more intelligent -> the most intelligent

<a id="b-three-degrees-of-adjective-comparison"></a>
## B. THREE DEGREES OF ADJECTIVE COMPARISON

<a id="i-positive-degree-fast-beautiful"></a>
### I. POSITIVE DEGREE: fast, beautiful
1.  **as + (short adjective/long adjective/adverb) + as**
    *   eg: *This car is as (fast/quick) as mine.* - *It runs as (fast/quickly) as mine.*
2.  **not so + (short adjective/long adjective/adverb) + as**
    *   eg: *French is not so difficult as English in the pronunciation.*
3.  **not as + (short adjective/long adjective/adverb) + as**

<a id="ii-comparative-degree"></a>
### II. COMPARATIVE DEGREE:
1.  **short adjective/adverb + er + (than)**
    *   eg: *My knife is sharper than yours.* - *You write faster/more quickly than I do. [quicker than: wrong]*
2.  **more + long adjective/adverb + (than)**
    *   eg: *You are more attractive than others.* - *You dance more attractively.*

<a id="iii-superlative-degree"></a>
### III. SUPERLATIVE DEGREE:
1.  **the + short adjective/adverb + est**
    *   eg: *He's the tallest in my class.* - *He came the earliest of others.*
2.  **the most + long adjective/adverb**
    *   eg: *I'm the most important in class.* - *He speaks English the most fluently.*
3.  **the least + long adjective/adverb**
    *   eg: *I'm the least important of my colleagues.* - *The new employee worked the least eagerly/efficiently.*

<a id="remarks"></a>
**Remarks:**
1.  Some adjectives have Latin comparatives:
    *   prior to, posterior to (anterior to), superior to, inferior to, senior to, junior to, major to, minor to.
    *   eg: *This event is anterior to (earlier than) that.*
    *   *That event is posterior to (later than) this.*
    *   *His authority is inferior to (less than) mine.*
    *   *My authority is superior to (greater than) his.*
    *   *His father is senior to (older than) my father.*
    *   *My father is junior to (younger than) his father.*
2.  Comparative degree is used between two items only.
    *   eg. *Tom is smarter than Richard.* (Not: Tom is smarter than other boys in his class.)
3.  Superlative degree is used among three or more items.
    *   eg. *Tom is the smartest boy in his class.* (Not: Tom is the smartest of any other boys in his class.)
4.  Irregular comparison. Some adj. adv. are compared in an irregular fashion.

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| little | less | the least |
| good, well | better | the best |
| much, many | more | the most |
| bad, ill, badly | worse | the worst |
| far | farther | the farthest |
| fore | former | the foremost, the first |
| near | nearer | the nearest, the next |
| old | later, latter / older, elder | the latest, the last / the oldest, the eldest |

<a id="c-double-comparatives"></a>
## C. DOUBLE COMPARATIVES
Double comparatives of adjectives follow the same rules of formation as those of simple comparatives.

<a id="one-adjective"></a>
**One adjective:**
1.  **Short adjective**: adj.+er and adj.+er (*The weather is getting colder and colder.*)
2.  **Long adjective**: more and more + long adj. (*My lessons become more and more difficult.*)
3.  **less and less + short adj./long adj.** (*It is less and less cold.*) (*The storm is less and less violent.*)

<a id="two-adjectives"></a>
**Two adjectives:**
1.  **With short adjectives**: The + adjective + er
    *   eg: *The higher the sun (is), the shorter the shadow (is).*
2.  **With long adjectives**: The more + adjective
    *   eg: *The more industrious he is, the wealthier he gets.*
3.  **With the verbs**: The more + subject + verb
    *   eg: *The more she sleeps, the more they want to annoy her.*
    *   *The more we talk peace, the more they want to make war.*
4.  **With the nouns**: The more + noun
    *   eg: *The more knowledge we have, the wiser we become.*
    *   *The more money he earns, the more debauched he is.*
5.  **Special case**: Sometimes we can reduce the statement by omitting the unnecessary words.
    *   eg: *The more (numerous we are), the merrier (we are).*
    *   *The more + V*: *The more he works, the more he earns.*
    *   *The less + S + V*: *The less he works, the less he will be paid.*
    *   *The sooner the better.*
    *   *The more he drank, the thirstier he was.*
    *   *The harder he tries, the less progress he seems to make.*

# Part 8: ACCENT MARKS

<a id="a-formation-of-words"></a>
## A. FORMATION OF WORDS
1.  **A root word**: a radical, base, fundamental combination of sounds forming a meaningful element of speech. eg: un - happi (y) - ness
2.  **A prefix**: a word or syllable placed in front of a word to add to or change the meaning of that word. eg: over (night/time), after (noon/word), under (clothes/ground), untrue, incorrect, discover
3.  **A suffix**: a letter or group of letters added at the end of a word to make another word.

<a id="b-guide-to-stress"></a>
## B. GUIDE TO STRESS
Stresses or accent marks are on nouns, adjectives, verbs and adverbs.
1.  Stress marks are usually placed on root words rather than on prefixes or suffixes. eg: speaker, unpleasant, behind, rewrite, already, impatient, reaction.
2.  In the case of two-syllable word:
    *   a/ stress marks are usually placed on the first syllable if the word is a noun or an adjective. eg: an accent, an export, a present, an import, an escort.
    *   b/ stress marks are usually placed on the second syllable if the word is a verb. eg: to accent, to export, to present, to import, to escort.
3.  As for words having 3 or more syllables, stress marks are usually placed on the third syllable counted backwards. eg: indicate, calendar, irregular, geography, activity, democracy, concentrate, variety, necessity.
4.  Stress marks are usually placed on the final syllables in words ending in ade, oo, oon, ee, eer, ese. eg: lemonade (=decade), balloon, refugee, engineer, Vietnamese.
5.  Stress marks are usually placed on the syllable next to the last one if the word ends in ic or i followed by a vowel.
    *   eg: -ic: characteristic, phonetics, athletics, mathematics (=arithmetic, politics).
    *   -j+v: artificial, musician, convenience, insufficient, familiar, satisfaction, religious.
6.  Stress marks are usually placed on the fourth syllable of words ending in ary counted backwards. eg: dictionary, ordinary, necessary, January (=elementary, supplementary).
7.  Stress marks are usually placed on the first element of compound nouns. eg: classroom, sitting-room, upstairs, armchair, school yard.
8.  Stress marks are usually placed over two elements of compound adjectives. eg: dark-brown, blue-black, ill-smelling, good-looking.

<a id="part-11-the-possessive-case"></a>
# Part 11: The Possessive Case

<a id="a-formation"></a>
## A. FORMATION
The possessive case is formed by adding `'s` or `'` after the noun possessor.

| Situation | Adding | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1/ Singular noun | `'s` | The coat of the boy → The boy's coat is too long for him. |
| 2/ Singular noun with s | `'` | The bicycle of Doris → Doris's bicycle is new. / The head of the ass → The ass's head / A fox's tail |
| 3/ Plural noun + s | `'` | The girls' books are wrapped in yellow nylon covers. |
| 4/ Plural noun (no 's' plural form) | `'s` | The men's club is located around the corner of the block. |

<a id="b-use"></a>
## B. USE
<a id="general-use"></a>
### General use
- **For persons:** He was confused by Mr Brown's looking so hard at him.
- **For animals:** Do you know why the camel's hump is so big? / Tom broke his dog's leg yesterday.
- **But not for things:** The leaves of the bamboos are curled by the heat. / The walls of that room are painted yellow. (That room's wall ❌)
- **But not for adjectives used as nouns:** The fate of the blind is miserable. / The park is the meeting place of the young.

<a id="special-use"></a>
### Special use
| Situation | Example |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Time | a vacation of three months → a three months' vacation |
| 2. Distance | a walk of two miles → a two-mile walk |
| 3. Weight | a box of ten pounds → a ten-pound box |
| 4. Value | a shirt of twelve dollars → a twelve-dollar shirt |
| 5. Dignified objects | The sun's energy - The earth's creatures - Heaven's will - The soul's consolation |
| 6. Personified objects | Fortune's smile - Sorrow's tears - Duty's call - The wind's hand - The thunder's roar. |
| 7. In familiar phrases | To my heart's contents - Get one's money's worth - For conscience's sake - The water's edge - For pity's sake - At his wits' end - Out of harm's way |

<a id="c-remarks"></a>
## C. REMARKS
1. **Compound nouns and noun phrases:** add the `'s` to the last word. (His father-in-law's property is quite big.)
2. **Several possessive nouns refer to the same noun, and imply common possession:** add the `'s` to the last word. (My brother and your sister's school has a good football team this year.)
3. **Different possessors are thought of separately:** add the `'s` to each word. (Ha's and Ba's bicycles collided at the street corner. / He likes to read both Dickens's and Hemingway's novels.)
4. **The possessive noun followed by an apposition:** add the `'s` to the apposition only. (This is my friend, Tom's new house. or This house is Tom's, my friend.)
5. **For the sake of euphony,** use `of` instead of `'s`. (This is the opinion of my wife's mother.)
6. **Noun used in the preceding clause is not repeated after the possessive case.** (I've seen your house, but not Mr. Brown's. / Dad's advice is the only advice I'll listen to.)
7. **Nouns denoting:** house, school, college, church, cathedral, shop, palace, hospital are often understood. (You can get it at the grocer's. / They eat dinner at my uncle's. / Tourists visited Notre Dame's.)
8. **Double possession:** This is one of my pictures. = This is a picture of mine. / That is one of Helen's friends. = That is a friend of Helen's.

# Part 13: The Five Basic Patterns of a Simple Sentence + Modifiers

The verb is a word which plays the most important part in the sentence. There are five kinds of verbs, so there are five basic patterns for the construction of a simple sentence.

**Pattern 1:** `S + Vi` (Intransitive Verb)
- The patient died.
- *With modifiers:* The wounded patient died painfully in this ward last night.

**Pattern 2:** `S + VL + SC` (Linking Verb + Subjective Complement)
- The child looks healthy.
- Milk looks very healthy.

**Pattern 3:** `S + Vt + DO` (Transitive Verb + Direct Object)
- Mr Brown bought a hat.
- *With modifiers:* Mr Brown, who lives near my house, bought an expensive new hat from that store.

**Pattern 4:** `S + Vt + IO + DO` or `S + Vt + DO + Prep. + IO`
- Her uncle gave the boy a bicycle.
- Her uncle gave a bicycle to the boy.
- *With modifiers:* Being very pleased, her uncle gave a red bicycle to the tallest boy.

**Pattern 5:** `S + Vt + DO + OC` (Transitive Verb + Direct Object + Objective Complement)
- The Committee elected my friend Secretary.
- *With modifiers:* The committee elected my friend Tom Secretary of the company.

<a id="part-15-present-past-participle-and-s-ed-endings"></a>
# Part 15: Present/Past Participle and "S"/"ED" Endings

<a id="a-how-to-form-the-present-participle"></a>
## A. HOW TO FORM THE PRESENT PARTICIPLE
**General rule:** Omit 'to' before adding suffix 'ing' to the verb. (to go → going; to be → being)
<a id="special-rules"></a>
**Special rules:**
1. Verbs ending with mute 'e': omit 'e' before adding 'ing'. (to write → writing; to come → coming)
2. Verbs ending with 'ie': turn 'ie' into 'y' before adding 'ing'. (to die → dying; to lie → lying)
3. One-syllable verbs formed with CVC: double final consonant before adding 'ing'. (to run → running; to stop → stopping; to quit → quitting)
4. Two-syllable verbs formed with CVC, stressed on second syllable: double final consonant. (to begin → beginning; to occur → occurring; to prefer → preferring)

<a id="b-how-to-form-the-past-participle"></a>
## B. HOW TO FORM THE PAST PARTICIPLE
**General rule:** Omit 'to' before adding past suffix 'ed' to regular verbs. (to help → helped)
<a id="special-rules"></a>
**Special rules:**
1. Regular verbs ending with mute 'e': omit 'e' before adding 'ed'. (to like → liked; to hope → hoped)
2. Regular verbs ending with 'y' after consonant: turn 'y' into 'i' before adding 'ed'. (to try → tried; to study → studied; to play → played)
3. One-syllable regular verbs formed with CVC: double final consonant before adding 'ed'. (to hop → hopped; to stop → stopped)
4. Two-syllable regular verbs formed with CVC, stressed on second syllable: double final consonant before adding 'ed'. (to occur → occurred; to happen → happened)

<a id="c-how-to-pronounce-the-s-ending"></a>
## C. HOW TO PRONOUNCE THE 'S' ENDING
1. Nouns/verbs ending with /k, p, t, f, θ/: add 's', pronounced /s/. (roofs, maps, months, likes, laughs)
2. Nouns/verbs ending with sibilant sounds /s, z, ʃ, tʃ, dʒ/: add 's/es', pronounced /ɪz/. (oranges, boxes, teaches, washes)
3. Nouns/verbs ending with other sounds: add 's', pronounced /z/. (stones, cities, rooms, bathes, studies)

<a id="d-how-to-pronounce-the-ed-ending"></a>
## D. HOW TO PRONOUNCE THE 'ED' ENDING
1. Verbs ending with /t, d/: add 'ed', pronounced /ɪd/. (wanted, divided, needed)
2. Verbs ending with /k, p, f, θ, s, ʃ, tʃ/: add 'ed', pronounced /t/. (placed, kicked, stopped, missed, laughed)
3. Verbs ending with /z, dʒ/ and other sounds: add 'ed', pronounced /d/. (opened, played, loved, prepared, raised)

<a id="part-17-the-simple-present-tense"></a>
# Part 17: The Simple Present Tense

<a id="form"></a>
**Form:**
1. Special verbs: auxiliary (am, is, are / has, have / do, does)
2. Defective verbs: can, may, must, ought to, shall, will
3. Ordinary verbs:
   - He, she, it: verb + s/es (to help → he helps / to go → he goes / to wash → he washes / to try → it tries)
   - I, you, we, they: verb (I help, you go, we wash, they try)

<a id="use"></a>
**Use:**
I. To express a present feeling or perception (a fact). (She's cold; I see a bird, She speaks English fluently.)
II. To express a general truth, an all-time truth (a natural phenomenon). (The sun rises in the east and sets in the west.)
III. To express a habitual action in the present (+ adverb of frequency). (They often go on a picnic.)
IV. To express a future action (+ time expression: next year, tomorrow). (I visit Hue next vacation. Your semester exam begins in three weeks.)

<a id="the-present-continuous-tense"></a>
## THE PRESENT CONTINUOUS TENSE
**Form:** Continuous form: to be + present participle (am/is/are + V-ing)
<a id="use"></a>
**Use:**
I. To express a continuous action at the moment of speaking (+ now, at present...). (Listen! Someone is knocking at the door.)
II. To express an action already begun before a repeated or habitual action in the present (+ while, when). (I never talk while I'm typing.)
III. To express two continuous actions happening at the same time. (He is working while his elder brothers are sleeping.)
IV. To express a near-future action that has already been arranged. (I am leaving tomorrow. She is coming to see me next week.)

**Remark:** We don't use continuous tense for verbs expressing:
1. a perception (see, hear, taste, smell, feel)
2. a status (seem, appear, look, be, become, grow)
3. an affection (love, hate, like, dislike, forgive, want, wish, desire, refuse)
4. a thinking (think, realize, understand, know, forget, remember, mind, expect, mean, notice)
5. possession (own, owe, belong, possess)
6. the beginning, continuation and ending of an action (begin, start, continue, stop, finish, end)

# Part 19: The Simple Future Tense

**Form:** I, we shall + verb / Other subjects: will + verb
<a id="use"></a>
**Use:**
1. To express an action or state in the future (pure future).
2. To express a polite request.
3. To express a future action in the form of the simple present tense in an adverbial clause of time, of condition and sometimes of purpose.
<a id="remarks"></a>
**Remarks:**
1. The simple future tense is never in an adverbial clause of time.
2. Other forms of future actions:
   - To be + going to + verb (future arrangement)
   - To be about to + verb (immediate future)
   - The simple present tense + time expression (near future)
   - The present continuous tense + time expression (future of intention)

<a id="the-future-continuous-tense"></a>
## THE FUTURE CONTINUOUS TENSE
**Form:** shall/will be + V-ing
**Use:** To express a continuous action at a point of future time.

**Use:**
1. To express an action which at some time in the past was regarded as future (reported speech).
2. To express a polite offer, invitation or request.
3. To express an obligation or advice; 'should' may be used with all persons.

<a id="notes"></a>
**Notes:**
1. Modal perfect with would, could or might generally implies the action was not carried out in the past or uncertainty.
2. Might contains less certainty than may.

<a id="modal-perfect-construction"></a>
**Modal Perfect Construction:**
I. **WOULD HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE** (Past-Unreal Condition)
II. **COULD HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE** (had been able to but didn't)
III. **MAY/ MIGHT HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE** (Possibility: Past / Uncertainty)
IV. **MUST + PERFECTIVE** (Deduction: Past / Certainty)
V. **SHOULD + PERFECTIVE / WAS/WERE SUPPOSED TO + V** (Unfulfilled obligation: Past)

<a id="dare-and-need"></a>
**DARE & NEED:**
- Dare: Defective verb (To venture) / Ordinary verb (To challenge)
- Need: Defective verb (in negative/interrogative) / Ordinary verb (+ noun)

<a id="part-27-the-passive-voice"></a>
# Part 27: The Passive Voice

**VOICE:** Active (subject acts) / Passive (something is done to subject).
<a id="formation"></a>
**FORMATION:**
STEP I: Finding out the finite verb → transform into `to be + Past participle`.
STEP II: Finding out the subject (doer) → put after finite verb with `by/with`. Omit if pronoun/people/someone.
STEP III: Finding out the object (receiver) → put at subject position. Agree with verb.
STEP IV: Finding out modifiers → adverb of definite time after `by + agent`, others before.

*Example:* Tom gives a speech this morning. → A speech is given by Tom this morning.
